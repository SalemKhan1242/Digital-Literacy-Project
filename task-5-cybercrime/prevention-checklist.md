# College Students Cyber Safety Checklist

##  Financial & UPI Safety

- **Verify the receiver before sending money:**  
  Always double-check the recipient’s name shown in the payment app before entering your UPI PIN. Scammers may use IDs that look like business accounts but are actually personal ones.

- **Never use your UPI PIN to receive payments:**  
  You only need your PIN to send money, not to receive it. If someone asks for your PIN for refunds, rewards, or prizes, it’s a scam.


##  Account & Device Security

- **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):**  
  Don’t rely only on passwords. Use authentication apps like Google Authenticator or Microsoft Authenticator for important accounts such as email, social media, and university portals.

- **Be careful with public Wi-Fi networks:**  
  Avoid logging into banking apps or sensitive accounts on public Wi-Fi (canteens, libraries, etc.). If needed, use a trusted VPN for added protection.


##  Academic & Career Protection

- **Stay alert for fake job offers:**  
  Be cautious of work-from-home offers on platforms like WhatsApp or Telegram promising high income for simple tasks.  
  Requests for registration fees or deposits are usually scams.

- **Access results only from official websites:**  
  Always confirm that you are using the official site when checking exam results. Fake websites may try to steal your credentials.


##  Privacy & Social Media Safety

- **Avoid oversharing personal details:**  
  Do not upload images of tickets, ID cards, or sensitive documents—even if partially hidden.  
  Also, avoid posting real-time location updates; share them only after leaving the place.

- **Check app permissions regularly:**  
  Some apps ask for unnecessary access to contacts, photos, or files.  
  Review and remove permissions for apps you don’t use or trust.
