# Case Study: The "Urgent Internship" Phishing Scam

## What is Phishing?

**Phishing** is a type of cyber attack where scammers impersonate a
trusted source---such as a university department, bank, or company
executive---to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information
like login credentials, personal identification details, or financial
data.

## How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step):

-   A student receives an email that appears to come from their
    university's Career Services office or a well-known professor. The
    subject line usually creates urgency, such as: **"URGENT: Paid
    Research Internship Opportunity."**

-   The message claims there are limited spots available and encourages
    the student to apply quickly through a provided link, often labeled
    as a "Google Form" or "Official University Portal."

-   When the student clicks the link, they are redirected to a fake
    login page designed to look almost identical to the real university
    website. As soon as they enter their username and password, the
    attacker captures these details instantly.

-   Using the stolen credentials, the attacker can access the student's
    real account, extract personal data, view academic or financial
    records, and even send similar phishing emails to others from the
    victim's account.

## Who is Targeted?

College students are especially vulnerable because they are actively
searching for internships and job opportunities and frequently receive
emails from academic and professional sources.

## The Consequences:

The effects can be serious. Victims may lose access to their accounts,
face identity theft, or suffer financial losses if their banking
information is linked. Additionally, their reputation may be harmed if
their account is used to scam others. Recovering from such an attack can
take significant time and effort, often involving multiple institutions.
